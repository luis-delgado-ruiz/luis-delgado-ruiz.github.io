3) Explain, in your own words, the three principles of system design
Early focus in users and tasks: En esta etapa se concentra únicamente en entender la idea del proyecto, el porqué, para qué, para quién. Igualmente conocer lo que el cliente desea de este mismo proyecto. Ya sabiendo esta información, planear el desarrollo de la página web y tener claras las tareas de cada persona(grupo) y sus prioridades individuales.
Empirical Measurement: In this stage it is important to have a feedback of the users to ensure that the project is going on the right direction. From there the webpage is tested and analyzed to fix errors.
Iterative design: As it is impossible to have a perfect product in the first run it is important to fix the errors found and repeat the process of testing, until the product works as desired.

4) Create your own methodology to develop a website. It’s important to justify all the steps.
Chat with the client and create wireframes: It is important to always have the customer feedback to ensure that their needs are met and that the final product behaves and sees as he wishes, from there create a visual guide of how the product form would be to be approved by the customer.
Planning: Once the development team knows the needs of the client we plan ahead our priorities and from there create sub-teams for different tasks and a schedule for the development process.
Prototypes: A fundamental element in the development process of a web page are the prototypes, not only to see if the product is coming as desired but also to test it and see its errors to fix them.
Validation of prototypes: As already said, it is vital that the client feels comfortable with the product and that it meets all their needs, just as it is important to have the validation of the development team that the project is on track as planned.
Development: Once the stages of prototyping and validation have been completed, the project can advance to the stage of development where there is already a more concrete vision of the final product.
Testing and feedback: Once the project is in its final stages it is very important to test it to ensure that it does not have bugs. At the same time, the client is presented with the almost finished version for his feedback and to make small changes if necessary.
Final tests and final validation: The last tests are done to ensure that the page works optimally and the finished product is presented to the client. It is always important that the client feels involved in the development process.
Delivery: The project is delivered to the customer.
